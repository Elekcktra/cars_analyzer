CARS Analyzer

The CARS Analyzer is a tool designed to bring structure and clarity to one of the most challenging components of MCAT preparation — the Critical Analysis and Reasoning Skills (CARS) section. Unlike traditional subjects, CARS is inherently qualitative. There are no formulas, and often no clearly “correct” reasoning path. The difficulty lies in understanding why an answer is wrong, rather than simply identifying the correct one. This makes consistent improvement difficult, as most review methods fail to capture patterns in thinking or reasoning errors over time. This project was originally built to support my girlfriend during her MCAT preparation, with the goal of turning a subjective review process into something more systematic, analyzable, and actionable.

What It Does

The CARS Analyzer processes and evaluates user-submitted explanations for incorrect answers, with a focus on identifying patterns in reasoning errors. Instead of treating each mistake in isolation, it organizes responses in a way that highlights recurring gaps in logic, comprehension, or interpretation. The objective is to move beyond answer-checking and toward a deeper understanding of how and why mistakes are being made.

How It Works

The application accepts an Excel file as input. The file must contain the following three columns:

Passage Name, Question Number, Why you think you got it wrong

Once uploaded, the tool analyzes the responses and generates structured feedback to help identify patterns across mistakes. This allows users to reflect more effectively on their reasoning and adjust their approach accordingly.

Objective

The goal of this tool is to improve the quality of CARS review by introducing structure into an otherwise subjective process. By focusing on reasoning patterns rather than individual outcomes, it helps users develop a more disciplined and consistent approach to tackling CARS questions.
